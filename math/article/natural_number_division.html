Division of Natural Numbers

Prerequisites

  • Natural Numbers
  • Multiplication of Natural Numbers

Informal description

If \(a\) objects are separated into \(b\) groups of the same size, the size of the groups is defined as the quotient of \(a\) and \(b\), also known as \(a\) divided by \(b\), denoted as \(a/b\). Taking the quotient of two numbers is known as division.

Not all pairs of natural numbers can be divided; for example, three cannot be divided by two.
