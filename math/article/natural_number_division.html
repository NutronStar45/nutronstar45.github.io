Division of Natural Numbers

Informal description

Prerequisites

  • Natural Numbers
  • Multiplication of Natural Numbers

When some objects are separated into a given number of groups of the same size, the size of the groups can be determined from the number of objects and the number of groups. If the number of objects is \(a\) and the number of groups is \(b\), the size of the groups is defined as the quotient of \(a\) and \(b\), also known as \(a\) divided by \(b\), denoted as \(a/b\). Taking the quotient of two numbers is known as division.

Not all pairs of natural numbers can be divided; for example, three cannot be divided by two.

Division can be defined through multiplication. If we separate a group of \(a\) objects into \(b\) groups of the same size, by the definition of multiplication,

\[ (a/b) \times b = a \]

If we swap \((a/b)\) and \(b\), the equation still holds true, and we arrive at another interpretation of division: "if a group of \(a\) objects is separated into groups of \(b\) objects, how many groups are there?"
