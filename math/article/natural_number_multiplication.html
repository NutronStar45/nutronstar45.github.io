Multiplication of Natural Numbers

Informal description

Prerequisites

  • Natural Numbers

Suppose we have some groups of the same size (e.g. boxes containing the same amount of fruit), then the total number of objects can be determined by the the size of the groups and the number of groups. If the size of the groups is \(a\) and the number of groups is \(b\), the total number of objects is defined as the product of \(a\) and \(b\), denoted as \(a \times b\). Taking the product of two numbers is known as multiplication.
