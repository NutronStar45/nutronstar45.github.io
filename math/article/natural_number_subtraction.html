Subtraction of Natural Numbers

Informal description

Prerequisites

  • Natural Numbers
  • Comparison of Natural Numbers
  • Addition of Natural Numbers

When some objects are removed from a group, the resulting group's size can be determined from the original size and the number of removed objects. If the original size is \(a\) and the number of removed objects is \(b\), the size of the final group is defined as the difference of \(a\) and \(b\), also known as \(a\) subtracted by \(b\), denoted as \(a-b\). Taking the difference of two numbers is known as subtraction.

In order for \(a-b\) to be a natural number, \(a\) must be greater than \(b\).

Subtraction can be defined through addition. If we take \(b\) objects from a group of \(a\) objects then put them back, the resulting group should still be \(a\) objects. Symbolically,

\[ (a-b) + b = a \]
