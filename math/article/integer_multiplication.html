Multiplication of Integers

Prerequisites

  • Integers
  • Multiplication of Natural Numbers
  • Absolute Value of Integers

Informal description

For integers \(a\) and \(b\), their product \(a \times b\) is defined to be

  • \(0\) if \(a=0\) or \(b=0\)
  • \(-(|a| \times b)\) if \(a<0\) and \(b>0\)
  • \(-(a \times |b|)\) if \(a>0\) and \(b<0\)
  • \(|a| \times |b|\) if \(a<0\) and \(b<0\)

Motivation

To extend multiplication to integers, we consider a reservoir whose amount of water is represented as a natural number (or zero). Suppose the amount of water increases by \(a\) every day, where \(a\) is an integer. We consider the difference of the amount \(b\) days later and that of today. The difference should be \(a \times b\) if \(a\) and \(b\) are nonnegative.
