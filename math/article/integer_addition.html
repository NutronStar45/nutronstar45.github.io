Informal description

For natural numbers \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\), the sum of \(a-b\) and \(b-c\) is defined to be \(a-c\).

Motivation

Adding differences of natural numbers combine them. Specifically, if \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\) are natural numbers with \(a>b>c\), then we have \((a-b) + (b-c) = a-c\). Integer addition is defined by generalizing this equation to cases where \(a>b>c\) doesn't hold.
