Addition of Integers

Informal description

Prerequisites

  • Integers
  • Natural Number Addition

Adding differences of natural numbers combine them. Specifically, if \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\) are natural numbers with \(a>b>c\), then we have \((a-b) + (b-c) = a-c\). We define integer addition by generalizing this equation to cases where \(a>b>c\) doesn't hold.

Like natural numbers, the sum of multiple integers does not depend on the the order of evaluation, so the sum can be written without parentheses; for example, \((a+b)+c\) can be written as \(a+b+c\).
