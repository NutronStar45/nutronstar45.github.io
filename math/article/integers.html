Integers

Informal description

Prerequisites

  • Natural Numbers
  • Subtraction of Natural Numbers

An integer is the difference of two natural numbers. Because the difference of two natural numbers isn't necessarily a natural number, not all integers are natural numbers. Integers are in either of these three categories:

  • Positive integers, the difference of a larger number and a smaller number
  • Negative integers, the difference of a smaller number and a larger number
  • Zero, the difference of two equal numbers

Positive integers are just natural numbers, and they are written the same way. Zero is written as 0. Negative integers have positive "sizes", for example \(3-5\) has the size of \(5-3=2\). In general, for natural numbers \(a\) and \(b\) with \(a < b\), the "size" of \(a-b\) is \(b-a\). Negative integers are written by adding a minus sign ("-") to the left of its "size", for example \(3-5=-2\).

Motivation

Sometimes we want to express the result of comparing two natural numbers. For example, consider a reservoir whose amount of water is represented by a natural number (or zero). The amount of water a reservoir gains in a day is the difference of the amount today and the amount yesterday. If the reservoir gains water, the difference is a natural number; if the reservoir loses water, the difference cannot be expressed as a natural number. This motivates the definition of integers.
